David M. Nisson

David M. Nisson is seeking to use existing coding skills for a new career in software development. David is working on full-stack projects for a diversity of applications and leveraging a variety of technologies. David's current projects are open-source and freely available.

David seeks to build web-based and desktop applications that are:

Responsive

David's apps will work with laptops, tablets, or smartphones.

Reliable

David's topmost priority is that his software can be counted upon to do its things correctly.

Fast

David doesn't like waiting for pages to load, and he doesn't want you to do the same.

Intuitive

David likes his interfaces to be easy to use and understand, and so he will build easy user interfaces for the software that he creates.

David was a graduate student researcher in physics, and in the course of his research wrote software for the University of California, Davis research groups to fulfill the needs of the researchers. It was during one of these projects that he realized his true passion for software development. Although this software belongs to UC Davis and is not publicly available, David is currently working on a number of publicly available full-stack projects.
